About Us

MoneyABCs.org is the University of Texas Arlington’s first financial literacy initiative with a nationwide focus. By creating the first professional network for educators, researchers, activists, regulators, and every organization actively engaged in designing and delivering financial literacy programs, we are taking a major step forward in the fulfillment of our mission of connecting individuals, institutions, and organizations to the resources and research they need in order to cultivate personal financial stability.

While many of its elements lend themselves well for general public consumption, this platform is intended to help those professionals who are actively engaged in financial literacy to freely share technical, pedagogic, human, and financial resources with each other in order to conduct their educational efforts more effectively and efficiently. Whether you are a K-12 educator working in a public or private school, whether you are a volunteer or paid-activists at a non-for-profit, or whether you work in the community outreach office of a depository institution, you are all welcome!

Join us if you want to gain free access to presentations, lectures, webinars, and videos with accompanying teaching notes and other supplemental materials. Join us if you would like to create a virtual course for your students or your target audience. Join us if you have educational resources you would like to share with other educators. Join us if you are in need or have financial resources to share with others. Join us if you would like to always stand at the frontier of knowledge in financial literacy by staying current on academic research and recent developments within the field. Join us if you wish to network with others in your community or across the nation. We want to help you connect and deliver your important message to the world!

Students

Welcome to MoneyABCs.org!
Whether you have a teacher guiding you in your studies, or whether you are learning on your own, our platform has something for you. Use our search engine to find and access learning materials on many financial topics of interest.

On our “Teach Me About Money” section, surely you will be able to find a video, webinar, podcast, or lecture that can help you understand the complex financial landscape better. Do you want to know which credit card is right for you? Do you want to know how to boost your credit score? Do you want to know how to secure a comfortable and laidback retirement? Are you already retired and you want to know how to manage and spend your retirement nest-egg? Try us out! Our platform is free and requires no sign-up for access.

Also, if you want to understand how the ever-changing financial landscape will affect you going forward, check out our “What’s in the News” section. There, you can find breaking news, editorials, analyses and thought pieces on any developments, trends, and policy changes within the field. There is no need to scout the whole internet for reliable information. Get it all her from reputable and trustworthy sources here.

Educators

MoneyABCs.org has been designed with educators in mind. It doesn’t matter whether you are a K-12 teacher, a college professor, a financial literacy volunteer trying to make a difference in your community, or whether you work in the community outreach office of a bank; our platform will help you find the resources you need to deliver your educational message more effectively and efficiently.

At MoneyABCs.org not only will you be able to find educational resources on many formats (PowerPoint, videos, podcasts, or webinars), but you will also be able to access teaching notes, discussion questions, lecture plans, supplementary activities, handouts, test banks, and useful feedback and recommendations from other members of the community. Not sure how to integrate financial literacy into your curriculum? Connect with other teachers in your grade-level, browse their lesson plans and replicate them with just a click. Not feeling confident enough to teach a specific topic? Call on other members of the network to secure a guest lecturer to come and speak directly to your class. Not sure if you have enough time in class to cover everything you want your students to learn? Structure an online course that your students can access and complete from home. Want to help others with their financial literacy programs, share your pedagogic materials within the platform and receive valuable feedback that you can use to improve your programs. The possibilities are endless!

In the future, schools, non-for-profits, and community activist will also be able to use the platform to raise funds to be able to carry on their educational mission. Whether you need just $50 to make handouts for one of your workshops or $10,000 to organize a literacy fair at your neighborhood community center, our network will help you connect with both public and private agents who will be happy to advance financial literacy by supporting your educational efforts.

Unfortunately, many K-College instructors, community volunteers, and non-for-profits often times struggle financially to be able to carry on with their educational missions. They know that their constituents need the information they can provide, but they do not have the funds to make it happen.

  • Ironically, there are many foundations, governmental agencies, corporations, and financial institutions who have resources to support financial literacy that sometimes go unused. The reasons behind this mismatch include lack of awareness from the community about the available funds, communication barriers between the users and providers of funds, limited outreach capacity by some of these parties, etc.
  • MoneyABCs.org will help bridge this gap by providing various services to its members, including the ability to find each other with the use of intuitive browsing and search engines that will allow all of them to search by a very diverse set of criteria, like educational objective, target audience, type of institution, geographical area, etc.

For those in need of funds:

  • Educators, researchers, and non-for-profits (with a diligent prior background check) will be able to set up a temporary, on online crowdfunding facility to either secure financing for individual programs, or to secure an operating budget for their various programs for a specific period in time.

For those in need of funds:

  • These crowdfunding facilities will allow these activists to raise the desired funds from either a small number of parties making large contributions, or through many supporters making small contributions.
  • The interested parties will be able submit their financial contributions over electronic wire, electronic check, or via a personal or corporate credit card.

For those in need of funds:

  • The recipients of funds will have the option of getting the funds wired to them once the fund-raising campaign is over, or to have UTA mail them a check when it is finished.
  • If the fund-raising campaign fails to raise enough funds to fulfill the intended objective of the program, the funds will be automatically reimbursed to the supporters of the campaign.

For those in need of funds:

  • The raised funds should only be used for the purposes outlined in the fund-raising page, and the recipients of the funds agree to submit an ex-post report of their program and activities, with pictures, numbers, and feedback from the individuals they served. This report will be posted in the platform so their supporters can be reassured that their money was put to good use.

For those in need of funds:

  • By the same token, the recipients of the funds agree to provide scanned receipts and other reasonable proof of their expenses to the platform for archival and accountability purposes. These receipts will not be made public, but will remain on file as an internal control and to attest to the accountability and transparency of the funds used.

For those in need of funds:

  • The recipients of funds will indicate upfront their willingness to comply with any additional reporting requirements the funder may have, like submitting any additional reports some depository institutions may need for receiving CRA credit in exchange for their financial support. When that happens, the platform will automatically facilitate the exchange of all necessary documentation between the sponsoring institution and the recipients of funds.

For those in need of funds:

  • The platform will make it easy for those agents in needs of funds to advertise their fund raising campaign on social media, like Facebook, LinkedIn, Twitter, or Instagram. The press of a button will be all that is needed to post the campaign to these popular, social-media outlets.

For those with available funds:

  • Those foundations, governmental agencies, corporations, and financial institutions who have the resources to support financial literacy will be able to set up a fund-granting facility through the platform.

For those with available funds:

  • This facility will allow them to promote within the platform their willingness to support other agents’ financial literacy programs, to indicate what types of programs they are willing to support, what are the requirements and standards for financial literacy activist to qualify for their grants, and what evidence of use of funds they will expect once the grant is given.

For those with available funds:

  • Those offering funds will have three options for their facility once the basic grant information is provided to the interested parties:
    • They can send interested parties to their own website to submit their applications there
    • They can make available in the platform a paper application that interested parties can print, complete, and mail over regular post, or
    • They can create an electronic form that will allow those interested in funds to submit their application directly through the MoneyABCs platform.

For those with available funds:

  • Once an applicant is granted funding, the funders will be able send the recipient the money electronically, or via a personal or corporate check.
  • The platform will automatically facilitate the exchange of all necessary documentation between the sponsoring institution and the recipients of funds to prove appropriate usage of funds.

For those with available funds:

  • The platform will make it easy for those offering financial support to promote their grants on social media, like Facebook, LinkedIn, Twitter, or Instagram. The press of a button will be all that is needed to promote these grants in these popular, social-media outlets.

Under Construction!

You will be able to find out what other members of the network who share profile attributes with you are doing to learn from their experience. For instance, if you are a fourth grade teacher, you can find out what individual videos, PowerPoint presentations, and webinars other fourth-grade teachers in your district, city, state, or across the nation are using in their classrooms

Similarly, if you are a financial literacy volunteer at a non-for-profit, if you are a community advocate, or a social security worker, you will be able to find out what other activists like you are doing in their communities and what pedagogic resources they are using.

You can also see the comments and feedback these users have left on specific videos or presentations they have used, and you can instant message them if they are online, leave a short message in their inbox, or email them directly to inquire about their experience with the main materials or with any the supplementary materials they have used (like teaching notes, or quizzes).

Aside from stand-alone materials, you will also be able to browse the virtual classrooms of users who have constructed such facilities within the platform. If after browsing the content and structure of their course you would like to replicate it for yourself, you will be able to do so simply with the click of a button.

By entering a specific area of expertise, license, certification, or background-check status, you will be able get a listing of the members of the community who meet such profile criteria so you can ask them for advise, or you can invite them to support your educational programs. For instance, if you are interested in finding a Certified Financial Planner who has a recent FBI clearance certificate on file with MoneyABCs.org to work with children within 20 miles of your elementary school’s address, you will be able to do so.

In this section of the dashboard you will be able to enhance your profile within the platform, to include your professional certifications, licenses, academic degrees, security clearance status, and your areas of expertise within the field of financial literacy. For instance, you can self-identify as a specialist in personal income taxes, in life insurance, or in investments. By the same token, you will be able to indicate whether you are willing to collaborate with others in your area, whether by travelling to the site of the event or by hosting people in your organization’s facilities.

To indicate your willingness to travel within your community, you will have the option of indicating which cities or zip codes you are willing to drive to. You can also just indicate how many miles around your contact address you are willing to travel, or manually draw, directly on the computer screen, over what area you are willing to travel within a map of your city.

You will also be able to share your daily, weekly, and monthly availability with others, so that they can see when they can count on your help for specific events they may be planning. If you chose to, you can also share your calendar of upcoming events with the community as a means to advertise them and get more attendance.

Finally, this is the section of the platform where you will be able to see any messages other members of the community have sent you through your platform. While some messages will be stored in your mailbox, you will also have the option to chat live with others if they are online at the same time as you. Eventually, users will be able to link their profiles to their smart phone, so they can get message notifications in real time directly to their smart phones, so they can stay better connected with others.

Do you want to share your white research paper, presentation slides, video, podcast, or webinar with other members of the community? Upload it to the platform using this form. Do not forget to also attach any supplementary resources that should accompany your file, like teaching notes, quiz questions, infographics, or handouts.

Under Construction!

Are you a K-College Instructor and would like to make some educational resources available online for your students to supplement what you are teaching in the classroom? Do you want to put together a self-contained course for your class that is delivered completely online? Are you a non-for-profit volunteer who would like to set up a series of online workshops for the community you serve? In MoneyABCs.org you will be able to do that and more. Here are some of the features our virtual classrooms will have.

  • You will be able construct your virtual classroom with a set of mixed educational resources (i.e. videos, webinars, podcasts, etc.) that your students can complete in a prescribed order or as they please.
  • For those courses with a prescribed order, your students will only be able to proceed to the next module after completing every aspect of one module (presentation, exercises, quizzes, etc.)
  • The modules can have an expiration date or be open for the students to complete at their leisure.
  • Your virtual classroom will be able to provide you with a universal username and password for all your students in case you do not wish to track your students’ progress or performance on an individual basis. However, you will also have the ability to provide each one of your students a unique username and password.
  • Usernames and passwords can be easily customized to be age appropriate, with visual passwords for small children and regular text-passwords for older students.
  • Your students’ privacy and security while participating in the platform will be safe-guarded by a master roster that you will be able to easily generate with the click of a button and store safely on your own computer. With no personally identifying data from your students ever uploaded onto our servers, you can rest assured that any potential malicious attacks on our servers will never comprise your students’ privacy or safety in any way.
  • You will have the opportunity to create exercises and quizzes (with follow-up essay questions, problems, or multiple-choice questions) that your students can complete and submit electronically.
  • To generate the exercises and quizzes on your virtual classroom, you will be able to use some of the existing questions in the platform’s exercise and test banks, but you will also be able to write your own exercises, problems, and quiz questions.
  • While the platform can grade problems and multiple-choice questions automatically, as the instructor you will have the opportunity to grade their essays on screen before the final grade of an exercise or quiz is generated.
  • You will have the option of deciding which exercises or quizzes will affect your student’s overall grade and which ones will serve just as practice runs or review.
  • Your students will be able to rate and leave comments on any or all the elements of your course, so you can use their feedback to judge which resources were most useful to them, and which resources were ineffective. Thus, with their feedback you will be able to improve your courses over time.
  • Your students will have the ability to send you questions, which you can answer privately over the platform, or you can publish on a digital billboard for everyone’s benefit. Thus, you will be able to create a FAQs section for your modules easily and in no time.
  • You will be able to customize the look of your virtual classroom, so that it reflects your institution’s colors, name, and even display your logo. Thus, your virtual classroom will feel like a part of your organization, rather than a facility provided by a third party. This feature should be somewhat reassuring for parents of small children who may have reservations about allowing their little ones interact with a third-party platform.
  • Your classroom will be assigned a URL that you can distribute to your students to allow them to access the virtual classroom directly without having to go through the front page of our website.
  • You will be able to maintain multiple courses at once in case you have different cohorts in an academic period, and you can replicate an entire course (or import just parts of it) easily with the click of a button.
  • You will have the option of sharing the courses you design with other educators in the network, or keep it private while you are beta-testing it.
  • You will be able to access our customer support free of charge if you ever encounter technical problems with your virtual classroom or if you are just learning how to use it.
